===SECTION B: OVERVIEW===
KeyQuestion: Why do elements have different properties?
Purpose: This lesson introduces students to the fundamental concept that all matter is composed of atoms, and that different elements have unique properties because they contain different numbers of protons, neutrons, and electrons. Students will explore element samples, develop atomic models, and connect this knowledge to materials used in Kenya.
Safety: Handle element samples carefully. Do not taste any samples. Wash hands after handling materials. Keep reactive metals away from water.

===SECTION C: FRAMEWORK===

ROW 1 - PREDICT:
Experience: Students examine 3-4 everyday materials (copper wire, iron nail, aluminum foil, carbon/charcoal) and predict why these materials look and behave differently despite all being solid metals or non-metals
Resources: Copper wire, iron nail, aluminum foil, charcoal pieces, observation worksheets
Teacher: Ask: "What makes copper different from iron?" "Why is aluminum shiny while charcoal is black?" "What do you think is inside these materials?" WAIT TIME: 10 seconds after each question
Sensemaking: Think-Pair-Share - Students think individually (1 min), discuss with partner (2 min), share with class (5 min)
Assessment: Prediction checklist - Did students make predictions? Are predictions based on observable properties? Did they participate in discussion?

ROW 2 - OBSERVE:
Experience: Students observe and test physical properties of element samples: color, luster, magnetism (using magnet), conductivity (simple circuit), malleability (gentle bending), and density (sink/float in water)
Resources: Element samples (Cu wire, Fe nail, Al foil, C/charcoal, S/sulfur powder), magnets, simple battery and bulb circuits, water containers, observation tables, hand lenses
Teacher: Circulate and facilitate observations. Ask: "What do you notice?" "How can you test if it conducts electricity?" "Which properties are similar? Different?" Guide safe handling
Sensemaking: Students record data in organized tables comparing properties of 4-5 elements. Groups share findings and create class data chart
Assessment: Observation criteria - Accurate data recording, systematic testing of properties, comparison of at least 3 properties per element, participation in group work

ROW 3 - EXPLAIN:
Experience: Students build explanations for why elements have different properties based on their observations. They discuss what might be "inside" each element that makes it unique
Resources: Class data chart from ROW 2, whiteboard, markers, periodic table poster
Teacher: Use questioning only - "Why do you think copper conducts electricity but sulfur doesn't?" "What could explain why iron is magnetic but aluminum isn't?" "What might be different inside each element?" Avoid giving answers directly
Sensemaking: Whole class discussion where students propose explanations. Teacher records ideas on board. Guide students toward the concept that different elements must have different internal structures
Assessment: Exit ticket - "Write one explanation for why elements have different properties" and "What question do you still have?"

ROW 4 - DQB:
Experience: Students generate questions about atoms and elements they want to investigate further. Focus on: What are atoms? How small are they? How many types exist? What's inside atoms?
Resources: Chart paper, markers, sticky notes for individual questions
Teacher: Record all questions without judgment. Organize questions into categories (structure, size, types, properties). Ask: "What else do you wonder about atoms?"
Sensemaking: Class creates a Driving Question Board. Vote on top 3 questions to investigate. Keep board visible throughout unit
Assessment: DQB participation - Each student contributes at least one question. Questions are recorded and organized on board

Sensemaking: Students create individual atomic models, then share in groups of 4. Identify common features and differences in models. Display models around classroom
Assessment: Model quality criteria - Model shows internal structure, includes labels, shows difference between elements, includes written explanation (3-4 sentences)
