GRADE 10 BIOLOGY (CBE)
STRAND: CELL BIOLOGY AND BIODIVERSITY
SUB-STRAND: CELL STRUCTURE AND SPECIALISATION
CBE Lesson Sequence v1 (Lessons 1-12)
Phenomenon-Driven with POE Cycles, DQB Tracking, Model Revisions, and Real Resource Links
These lesson plans guide Grade 10 learners through understanding cell structure, microscopy, cell specialization, and levels of organization. The sequence uses wound healing as an anchoring phenomenon to drive student investigation and sense-making.

Focus for Lessons	This unit guides learners to understand that all living things are made of cells, that cells have specific structures with functions, that cells can be specialized for different roles, and that cells organize into tissues, organs, and systems.
Total Duration	40 periods (12 core lessons including practicals and assessments)
Anchoring Phenomenon	WOUND HEALING: When you get a cut on your skin, it bleeds, hurts, and looks damaged. But after a few days, new skin grows back and the wound closes. How does your body grow new skin? Where does the new skin come from? What’s happening that we can’t see?Students observe time-lapse videos of wounds healing and wonder about the invisible processes creating new tissue. This phenomenon is personal (everyone has experienced it), observable, puzzling, and can be explained through cell biology.
Storyline Thread	Students begin puzzled by how wounds heal - where does new skin come from? Through investigation, they discover that all living things are made of tiny cells (too small to see), that microscopes allow us to see cells, that cells have complex internal structures, that different cells have different shapes and functions (specialization), and that cells work together in organized levels (tissues → organs → systems) to perform complex functions like healing. They learn that understanding cells explains not just healing but growth, reproduction, and all life processes.
Key Inquiry Questions / Driving Questions	1. Main Driving Question: How does your body heal a wound? Where does new skin come from?2. What are living things made of?3. How can we see cells?4. What do cells look like inside?5. Are all cells the same?6. Why are cells different shapes?7. How do cells work together?8. How does understanding cells help us in Kenya?
